Recovery Guide

You Can Quit.
Here's How.

Step-by-step guidance for quitting smoking — practical strategies, recovery advice, motivational benefits, and support resources. It won't be easy, but it's the best decision you'll ever make.

Get Support See Benefits ↓
Step-by-Step

Your Quit Plan

01
Set a Quit Date

Choose a specific date within 2 weeks. Mark it on your calendar and fully commit to it.

02
Reduce Gradually

Start cutting down cigarettes per day. Gradual reduction makes the final quit less overwhelming.

03
Avoid Triggers

Identify situations that trigger cravings — stress, routines, social settings — and plan around them.

04
Seek Support

Tell family and friends. Contact a helpline or cessation programme. Don't do it alone.

05
Adopt Healthy Habits

Replace smoking with exercise, hobbies, or healthy snacks. Keep hands and mind occupied.

Benefits

What Quitting Does for Your Body

Time After QuittingWhat Happens in Your Body
20 minutesHeart rate and blood pressure begin dropping to normal
12 hoursCarbon monoxide in blood returns to normal; oxygen levels rise
2–12 weeksCirculation improves; lung function increases noticeably
1–9 monthsCoughing and shortness of breath decrease significantly
1 yearHeart disease risk is halved compared to a smoker
5 yearsStroke risk drops to that of a non-smoker
10 yearsLung cancer risk halved vs a continuing smoker
15 yearsHeart disease risk equals someone who has never smoked

Quitting leads to better breathing and a longer, healthier life

More Reasons

Additional Benefits of Quitting

  • Improved breathing and increased energy levels
  • Better-smelling breath, hair, and clothes
  • Improved sense of taste and smell
  • Healthier skin — reduced premature ageing
  • Significant financial savings over time
  • Reduced risk of cancer, heart disease, and stroke
  • Longer life expectancy
  • Protecting family and others from passive smoke
