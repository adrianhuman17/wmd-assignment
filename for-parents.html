Practical Advice

Tips for Parents

💬
Use Effective Communication Techniques

Discuss smoking before your child encounters it. Use age-appropriate language and remain open, calm, and non-judgmental. Children as young as 8 can understand the risks when explained clearly.

👀
Be a Positive Role Model

If you smoke, consider quitting. Children of non-smoking parents are 3× less likely to start. Your behaviour is the most powerful influence on your child's choices.

📚
Educate, Don't Just Forbid

Explain nicotine addiction in age-appropriate terms. Knowledge and open discussion are far more effective deterrents than strict prohibition, which can increase curiosity.

🔍
Recognise Warning Signs

Watch for: smell of tobacco or mint gum, unexplained coughing, withdrawal from family, unexplained expenses, or possessing lighters or vaping devices. Address concerns early and calmly.

🏠
Create a Supportive Home Environment

Keep your home and car smoke-free. Encourage healthy activities and hobbies. Build trust so your child feels safe discussing peer pressure situations with you.

Conversation Starters

How to Start the Conversation

For Children (Ages 8–12)
  • "Did you know that smoking can make people very sick — even children who just breathe the smoke?"
  • "What would you do if a friend offered you a cigarette?"
  • "Why do you think some people smoke even though they know it's harmful?"
For Teenagers (Ages 13–17)
  • "Have any of your friends started vaping or smoking? What do you think about that?"
  • "Did you know tobacco companies specifically target young people in their advertising?"
  • "If you felt pressure to smoke, how would you handle it? How can I support you?"
"The greatest protection a parent can give their children against smoking is to model a smoke-free life." — Health Professionals Advisory
