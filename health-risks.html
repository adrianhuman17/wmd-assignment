Health Education

Smoking Kills —
Here's How

The effects of tobacco on the human body are severe, progressive, and often fatal. Smoking damages nearly every organ system. These are the facts, supported by global health research.

Get Help to Quit →
Body Systems

Effects by Body System

🫁 Respiratory System

Lung cancer, COPD, chronic bronchitis, emphysema. Smoking permanently destroys lung tissue. Smokers are 25× more likely to develop lung cancer than non-smokers.

❤️ Cardiovascular System

Heart disease, stroke, arterial hardening, and blood clots. Smokers are 2× more likely to suffer a heart attack. Nicotine raises blood pressure and heart rate significantly.

🧠 Brain & Nervous System

Significantly increases stroke risk. Nicotine alters brain chemistry, creating powerful dependency and severe withdrawal symptoms when quitting.

🦷 Mouth, Teeth & Throat

Oral cancer, gum disease, tooth discolouration and loss, persistent bad breath — often the earliest visible signs of tobacco damage on the body.

Passive Smoking

Second-Hand Smoke

Non-smokers exposed to second-hand smoke face nearly the same health risks as active smokers. Children are especially vulnerable as their organs are still developing.

  • Increased risk of lung cancer in non-smokers
  • Asthma and respiratory conditions in children
  • Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS)
  • Heart disease risk increases by 25–30%
  • Ear infections in young children
  • Reduced lung growth and function in children

Second-hand smoke is dangerous for the whole family
