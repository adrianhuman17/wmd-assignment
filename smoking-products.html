All forms of smoking are harmful. Learn how each product works, what it contains, and how it affects your health. No form of smoking is safe.

Types of Smoking Products

Most CommonTobacco
Cigarettes

Rolled tobacco smoked through a filter. Contains nicotine, tar, and 7,000+ chemicals — at least 70 cause cancer. Highly addictive and the leading preventable cause of death worldwide.

Tobacco
Cigars

Rolled bundles of fermented tobacco leaf. Contain significantly more nicotine per use than cigarettes. Carry all the same toxic and carcinogenic compounds as cigarette smoke.

ElectronicYouth Risk
Vapes / E-cigarettes

Battery-powered devices heating liquid nicotine into aerosol. Often flavoured to attract younger users. Still highly addictive and harmful despite being marketed as "safer."

SocialTobacco
Hookah / Hubbly

Water pipe for smoking flavoured tobacco. One session equals 100+ cigarettes of smoke. A greatly underestimated health risk in social settings.

Passive Smoking

Passive smoking — also called second-hand smoke — occurs when non-smokers breathe in smoke from nearby smokers. It is just as dangerous as active smoking and particularly harmful to children whose organs are still developing.

  • Increases lung cancer risk in non-smokers
  • Causes asthma and respiratory conditions in children
  • Linked to Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS)
  • Raises heart disease risk by 25–30%
  • Ear infections in young children
